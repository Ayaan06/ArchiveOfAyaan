About Me

From building AI models that can identify tumors in medical images to developing blockchain systems that automate trading, I'm passionate about creating technology that doesn't just work, but matters.

I am pursuing a Bachelor of Computing (Honours Co-op) in Computer Science with a Project Management minor at the University of Guelph. I have completed multiple certifications including Microsoft Azure (AZ-900, AI-900), AWS Cloud Practitioner, and CompTIA Security+ and mastered tools like Python, C#, C++, Java, Swift, and Power BI. What drives me most is discovering the real-world impact behind these skills.

I created an AI-powered tumor detection bot using Azure Machine Learning Studio, PyTorch, and TensorFlow. The model analyzes MRI and CT scans to detect and classify tumors, applying the concepts from my AI-900 certification to demonstrate how artificial intelligence can contribute to healthcare innovation.

Alongside that, I engineered SolSync, a blockchain-based trading bot on the Solana network that mirrors trader transactions in real time. By utilizing WebSockets, Web3.py, RPC APIs, and Python, I achieved near-instant execution and a 99% success rate, discovering an entirely new way to trade crypto efficiently and securely.

Professionally, I have worked as a Software Engineer Intern with Apple x OurWaveHub, developing and refining an iOS app while learning to collaborate effectively within agile development teams. At the Town of Oakville, I served as an Environmental Impact Analyst, transforming municipal sustainability data into actionable insights.

I thrive at the intersection of AI, cloud computing, and software engineering where innovative ideas evolve into systems that truly make a difference. I'm constantly pushing the boundaries of what I can build next and look forward to applying this mindset to new opportunities. Check out my GitHub for more open-source projects I have built.
